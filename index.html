About this tracker

PH Fuel Tracker displays DOE Philippines weekly retail petroleum price monitoring data. It mirrors the official DOE OIMB format: overall range (min–max) and common price per product, brand-agnostic.

Data Sources

· DOE OIMB weekly price monitoring PDFs — prod-cms.doe.gov.ph

· DOE oil monitor portal — doe.gov.ph/oil-monitor

· CEIC Philippines Energy Database (monthly NCR averages, 2017–2024)

· GlobalPetrolPrices.com (cross-verification)


Key Policy Events

· Jan 1, 2018 — TRAIN Law (RA 10963): +₱2.65/L gasoline excise, +₱2.80/L diesel excise

· Jan 1, 2019 — TRAIN 2nd tranche: +₱1.40/L gasoline, +₱2.00/L diesel

· Apr–May 2020 — COVID-19; historic lows; WTI crude negative Apr 20, 2020

· Feb–Jun 2022 — Russia-Ukraine war; diesel peaked ~₱86/L NCR

· Feb 28, 2026 — Strait of Hormuz closure (Iran war); 20% of world oil supply disrupted

· Mar 24, 2026 — President Marcos declares national energy emergency

· Apr 7–13, 2026 — Diesel peaked ₱153.70/L NCR common (Rappler/DOE)

· Apr 14, 2026 — Rollback 1: diesel −₱20/L → ₱133.70; DOE PDF confirms ₱123.40 common Apr 14-20

· Apr 21, 2026 — Rollback 2: diesel −₱24.94/L, RON91 −₱3.41/L (announced by Marcos Apr 18)


Methodology

· 2017–2024: DOE monthly NCR averages from CEIC + DOE annual situationers

· 2025–2026: DOE weekly PDF monitoring reports (prod-cms.doe.gov.ph)

· Per-brand prices: DOE NCR common price ± observed brand premium/discount from DOE monitoring tables

· Regional prices: NCR common price + DOE per-region monitoring differentials

· Auto-updated every Tuesday via GitHub Actions after DOE advisory
